Introduction to the Program
Chemistry involves the study of substances, their properties, structures, reactions and
transformations. Chemistry is interlinked with various scientific disciplines and play an
important role in our daily life. It broadly covers various areas including medicinal
chemistry, analytical chemistry, organometallic chemistry, polymer chemistry, quantum
chemistry, catalysis and many aspects of materials science. In the modern society,
knowledge of Chemistry is essential for addressing most of the real-life challenges related
to water, energy, food, agriculture, security and healthcare.
Objectives of the Program
The major objective of the program is to bring out professionals having fundamental
knowledge, and specific skill sets of various aspects of chemistry such as Organic
Reactions and their Mechanism, Main-Group and Transition Metals, Organometallics and
Catalysis, Physical and Spectroscopic techniques, Inorganic and Solid State Material
synthesis, Computational and Theoretical Chemistry.
The department focuses on preparing future students to take up challenges at various
levels of academics, R&Ds and industries.
Expected Graduate Attributes
(1) Capability to appreciate synthetic methods for development of important chemical
products such as pharmaceutical drugs, paints, petrochemicals, food additives, plastics
etc.
(2) Ability to use characterization tools for analysing reactions and monitoring chemical
processes for product development.
(3) Ability to handle analytical, spectroscopic, microscopic and other instrumental
methods for interdisciplinary research areas.
(4) Use of computational tools to understand and analyse chemical and biophysical
processes, molecular structures and advanced materials.
(5) Ability to comprehend, write and present scientific articles and technical reports.
(6) Develop oral and written communication skills.
(7) Appreciation and adherence to norms of professional ethics and sense of intellectual
integrity.
(8) Ability to plan and manage technical research projects and work in a team.
Program Learning outcome
(1) Strong fundamentals in theoretical and practical aspects of organic, inorganic and
physical chemistry.
(2) Develop appreciation of hands-on wet chemistry, analytical, instrumentation, and
experimental data handling skills to solve scientific problems to address technological
challenges in various domains.
(3) Knowledge of computational and theoretical chemistry.
(4) Skills for material synthesis and processes for device integration
(5) Develop the ability to exercise independent learning, thinking and problem solving
skills.
(6) Develop the ability to interact in a cross-cultural environment and acquire time and
project management skills for carrying out guided research work in any of the research
area related to science and technology.
(7) Develop skills to communicate scientific learning and present ideas.

Objectives
The instructor will:
1. To give students a better insight into the mechanism of diverse set of organic reactions

and selectivity of organic reactions.
Learning Outcomes
The students are expected to:
1. The students are expected to be able to draw mechanisms for complex reactions
2. Understand and predict the orbital interactions involved in organic reactions
Contents
Introduction: reaction mechanism and its need, methods of determination of reaction
mechanism, concerted versus stepwise processes, transition states and intermediates,
early and late transition states, crossover experiments and examples, molecular orbital
interaction of common functional groups (7 lectures)
Acids and bases: the pKa scale and its interpretation, pKa‟s of common acids; specific and
general acid and base catalysis (3 lectures)
Classification of reactions: introduction to substitution, elimination and addition reactions,
aromaticity and aromatic substitution reactions (9 lectures)
Stereochemistry: basics of Stereochemistry, stereochemistry of aliphatic compounds,
conformational analysis of acyclic and cyclic compounds, (6 lectures)
Selectivity: chemoselectivity, regioselctivity, and stereoselectivity (4 lectures)
Radical Chemistry: Basics of radicals, radical initiators, radical halogenation of alkanes,
radical name reactions (6 lectures)
Rearrangements and migrations: cationic rearrangements, carbanionic rearrangements,
carbene/carbenoid based rearrangements (7 lectures)

Title Isolation and Synthesis of Natural Products Number CYL7XX0
Department Chemistry L-T-P [C] 3–0–0 [3]
Offered for Type Elective
Prerequisite
Objectives
The instructor will:
1. Give students a brief history of natural products, their isolation and synthetic studies.
2. Students will learn about multiple synthetic approaches to natural products including
synthetic drugs.
3. Students should understand the complete process of total synthesis of natural products and
effort required to this end.
Learning Outcomes
The students are expected to:
1. Understand advanced retrosynthetic principles (e.g. key disconnection, chiral pool starting
materials, reactive functional group) and apply in the synthesis including stereochemical
aspects
2. Understand key principles behind enantio- and diastereoselectivity, and develop skills to
predict and interpret stereochemical outcome of reactions.
3. Learn method development and their application in the total synthesis of natural products in
an economical way.
Contents
Introduction: Types of natural products - classification - terpenoids, steroids, alkaloids, natural
pigments, carbohydrates etc. (3 lectures)
Isolation: Isolation technique with examples and preliminary identification of natural products.
(2 lectures)
Retrosynthesis and synthetic routes: Logical retrosynthetic disconnection, method
developments and applications in the total synthesis of natural products. (8 lectures)
Recapitulation of stereochemical concepts: stereoselective and stereospecific reactions,
prochirality, Chemo-, regio-, diastereo- and enantio-controlled approaches; chirality transfer,
asymmetric inductions; chiral pools, chiral auxiliaries, chiral catalysts. (6 lectures)
Terpenoids : Classification, structure, chemistry and biogenesis; Steroids : Sterols and bile
acids, estrogens, androgens, gestogens and adrenocortical hormones; Alkaloids (5 lectures)
Characteristic reactions, general methods of degradation, structure and chemistry of some
well-known alkaloids; Natural Pigments: Flavones, flavanones, isoflavones, xanthones,
quinones, pterins, chlorophyll and haemin; and Carbohydrates. (8 lectures)
Synthesis of biologically active Natural products: Strychnine (Overmann Syn.), Fredrikamycin
A(Norman and Coxan), Cephalosporin (Woodward), Estrone, Mifepristone (Vollard), Taxol,
Tubulysin, Epothilone, Structure determination of Natural products: Arteannuin B, Hardwickiic
acid, and Ajmaline, stereoselective synthesis of Ibuprofen, crixivan, propranol. (10 lectures)

Title Stereochemistry of Organic Compounds Number CYL7XX0
Department Chemistry L-T-P [C] 3-0-0 [3]

Prerequisite
Objectives
The instructor will:
1. Provide understanding of representing stereochemical structures of organic molecules.

Learning Outcomes
The students are expected to have the ability to:
1. Understand stereochemical nomenclature, classification of objects with regard to
symmetry, desymmetrizations, stereochemical discrimination, separation of

and diastereomers (3 lectures)
Prostereoisomerism, Prochirality: Homotopic and heterotopic ligands, hetrotopicity and NMR,
prochirality and centers (4 lectures)
Conformation of Acyclic and cyclic Molecules: Conformation of Acyclic molecule, Physical and
spectral properties of diastereomers and conformers, Conformational aspects of various ring
size compound, stereochemistry of fused and bridged compounds (10 lectures)
Stereoselective Synthesis: Convergent Synthesis, Distereoselective synthesis,
Enantioselective synthesis and double stero-differentations (8 lectures)
Chiroptical Properties: Optical activity, Anisotropic Refraction, Circular dichroism, application

modern and emergent technologies, modern mathematics coupled with computational
capabilities is opening new avenues for further explorations. M.Sc. (Mathematics)
Program at IIT Jodhpur aims at tapping these opportunities by developing workforce
specialized in mathematics needed for developing key technologies. The proposed four
semester program strives to provide balance between analysis and application of
Mathematics. The compulsory courses provide the basic understanding and foundation of
mathematics enhancing analytic capabilities of the students. Students can further
explore their area of interest with program and open elective courses. The project
component spread over last two semesters provides research exposure to the students

problems. Students are encouraged to undertake research Summer Internships in
Institute of repute, Industry and R&D organizations.

Introduction
The digital revolution of the 20th and 21st centuries has created new knowledge and
spaces for humankind to evolve culturally with the construction of techniques and
technologies. As a corollary, a new field of organised research emerged at the beginning
of the 2000s examining the use and application of digital technologies in the humanities,
liberal arts, social science scholarship, computer sciences, and beyond. Popularly nested
under an umbrella term “Digital Humanities”, this area of scholarship takes a critical
stance to examine the role, use, application, and impact of digital tools in our everyday
life, our societies, economies, cultures, and governments. However, while the use of
computational tools in social science and humanities work is not very new, the
availability of a large body of cultural artefacts after the digital turn, as well as
emergence of new kinds of digital objects and embodiments, has opened up several
possibilities for social science and humanities research, practice and pedagogy using
computational approaches. Very soon, the question of whether something is, or is not,
„digital‟ will be increasingly secondary as many forms of culture become mediated,
produced, accessed, distributed or consumed through digital devices and technologies.
In other words, the field of Digital Humanities is expected to grow on
interdisciplinaryrather than multidisciplinary principles. The research focus and pedagogy
in this program emphasize many of the emerging epistemological questions on
knowledge production about generating digital data from material objects, and on
rethinking existing processes of knowledge production.
Objective of the Program
IIT Jodhpur is one of the pioneering institutes in the country currently offering an
interdisciplinary doctoral degree in Digital Humanities. This doctoral program offers
unique opportunities to redraw conventional disciplinary boundaries among the
humanities, the social sciences, the arts, technology and engineering, and the natural
sciences. Primary objectives of the program include (but are not limited to):
1. Introducing and exploring a broad spectrum of perspectives on the Digital
Humanities
2. Developing familiarity with a range of Digital Humanities projects, as well as the
ability to evaluate the tools and methods involved in creating and analysing those
projects
3. Critical and reflexive use of digital tools, technologies, and spaces by
understanding that all technologies are complex and socially situated, through
which humans make meaning
Doctoral students opting for this program are expected to contribute towards integrating
disciplinary approaches of the humanities, liberal arts, social sciences, and computer
technologies, with cross-disciplinary theorising and research that can be avant-garde to
103
the field of Digital Humanities. Doctoral research in Digital Humanities shall be premised
on project-oriented knowledge production, the practical application of methods, and shall
involve interdisciplinary collaboration workflows. To enhance such collaborative
workflows, the programme will actively involve academics and resource people from
other institutes and universities across the country and internationally.
Expected Graduate Attributes
In depth understanding of the fundamental concepts of broad areas in Humanities, Social
Sciences, and application areas of basic computational tools.
1. Ability to undertake problems in multidisciplinary domains of Humanities, Social
Sciences, Computer Sciences, Mathematics, and related disciplines.
2. Skills to demonstrate basic principles of Humanities and Social Sciences through
various application areas and analytical processes.
3. Ability to make effective oral and written technical communication.
4. Appreciation and adherence to norms of professional ethics.
Learning Outcomes
1. Appreciate and critically discuss Digital Humanities projects in light of current
methods and theoretical approaches to the field
2. Explain how Digital Humanities methods and practices are applied in different
disciplines
3. Skillset to plan, develop, and evaluate a Digital Humanities research project
4. Fundamental understanding of research tools to plan for and evaluate the
effectiveness of Digital Humanities methods including a multitude of platforms
such as social media and web tools.
5. Understand what data mean in different contexts
6. Capture, collect, and analyse a variety of data types related to Digital Humanities
research project

 Creating a platform to use various electronic, mechanical, and bio-sensors for
improving the remote health care facilities
